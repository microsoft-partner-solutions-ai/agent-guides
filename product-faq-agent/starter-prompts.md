# Starter Prompts for Product FAQ Agent

These prompts help users understand the agent's capabilities and get started quickly. Customize these prompts with your specific product names and features.

## Basic Product Information

### Prompt 1: Product Overview
**User Input:**
```
What are the main features of [Product Name]?
```
**Expected Outcome:** The agent provides a comprehensive list of key product features with brief descriptions, helping users understand the product's core capabilities.

**Example Variations:**
- "Tell me about [Product Name]"
- "What can [Product Name] do?"
- "Give me an overview of [Product Name]"

---

## Tips for Creating Your Own Prompts

### General Guidelines

1. **Be Specific**: Include product names, feature names, or specific tasks
2. **Use Natural Language**: Write as you would naturally ask the question
3. **Start Simple**: Begin with basic questions before moving to complex queries
4. **Include Context**: Mention your plan, version, or use case when relevant
5. **Try Variations**: The agent can understand different phrasings of the same question

### Effective Prompt Patterns

**For How-To Questions:**
- "How do I [action]?"
- "What are the steps to [task]?"
- "Guide me through [process]"

**For Information Queries:**
- "What is [feature/term]?"
- "Tell me about [topic]"
- "Explain [concept]"

**For Troubleshooting:**
- "I can't [action]"
- "Why is [issue happening]?"
- "How do I fix [problem]?"

**For Comparisons:**
- "What's the difference between [A] and [B]?"
- "Should I use [option A] or [option B]?"
- "Compare [item A] with [item B]"

**For Recommendations:**
- "Which [option] is best for [use case]?"
- "What do you recommend for [scenario]?"
- "What's the best way to [accomplish goal]?"

### What Makes a Good Starter Prompt

A good starter prompt should:
- ✅ Be clear and specific
- ✅ Represent common user questions
- ✅ Showcase different agent capabilities
- ✅ Be immediately useful to new users
- ✅ Include realistic use cases
- ✅ Cover various difficulty levels (basic to advanced)

### What to Avoid

Avoid prompts that:
- ❌ Are too vague ("Tell me everything")
- ❌ Require information outside the knowledge base
- ❌ Ask for personal account details
- ❌ Request actions the agent can't perform
- ❌ Include internal jargon users wouldn't know

### Testing Your Prompts

Before finalizing starter prompts:
1. Test each prompt with the agent
2. Verify the response is accurate and helpful
3. Ensure the agent has the knowledge to answer
4. Check that responses match expected outcomes
5. Refine prompts based on actual performance

## Customization for Your Product

When adapting these prompts for your specific product:

1. **Replace Placeholders**: Change [Product Name], [Feature], etc. to your actual product names
2. **Add Product-Specific Terms**: Include your unique features and terminology
3. **Align with Customer Language**: Use terms your customers actually use
4. **Focus on Common Questions**: Prioritize questions you frequently receive
5. **Match Your Use Cases**: Highlight scenarios relevant to your customer base
6. **Keep It Current**: Update prompts when product features change
